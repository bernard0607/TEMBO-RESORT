Top 10 Must-Visit Attractions in Nairobi

August 15, 2023 By Stardom Team Travel Guide

Nairobi, Kenya's vibrant capital, is a city of contrasts where modern skyscrapers stand alongside sprawling national parks. Whether you're a nature enthusiast, culture vulture, or history buff, Nairobi offers an array of unforgettable experiences. Here are the top 10 must-visit attractions in and around the city:

1. Nairobi National Park

Experience the unique thrill of spotting lions, rhinos, and giraffes with the city skyline as your backdrop at Nairobi National Park. This remarkable wildlife haven is just 7km from the city center, making it the world's only national park bordering a capital city. Don't miss the Ivory Burning Site Monument and the animal orphanage within the park.

2. David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust

Witness the incredible work being done to protect orphaned elephants at this world-renowned elephant orphanage. The daily public visiting hour (11am-12pm) allows you to watch the baby elephants being fed and playing in the mud. It's a heartwarming experience that supports vital conservation efforts.

3. Giraffe Centre

Get up close and personal with endangered Rothschild's giraffes at this conservation center. You can hand-feed these gentle giants from a raised platform, making it a fantastic experience for visitors of all ages. The center also offers nature trails through the adjacent Gogo River bird sanctuary.

4. Karen Blixen Museum

Step back in time at the former home of the famous Danish author of "Out of Africa." The museum offers guided tours of the colonial-era house and its beautiful gardens, providing insight into Kenya's colonial history and the life of this remarkable woman.

5. Bomas of Kenya

Immerse yourself in Kenya's rich cultural heritage at this living museum. The traditional homesteads (bomas) represent the country's major ethnic groups, and the daily performances of traditional dances and music are a highlight not to be missed.

6. Karura Forest

Escape the city bustle in this urban forest reserve, perfect for walking, cycling, or picnicking. The forest features waterfalls, caves, and over 50km of trails, offering a peaceful retreat and excellent birdwatching opportunities.

7. Nairobi National Museum

Discover Kenya's rich natural and cultural history through fascinating exhibits ranging from early human fossils to contemporary art. The museum complex also includes a snake park and botanical gardens.

8. Kazuri Beads Women's Cooperative

Visit this inspiring social enterprise that provides employment opportunities for single mothers. Take a tour to see how the beautiful ceramic beads are made, and browse the colorful jewelry and pottery in the gift shop.

9. Ngong Hills

For breathtaking panoramic views of the Great Rift Valley and Nairobi, hike the scenic Ngong Hills. The gentle trails are suitable for most fitness levels, and you might even spot some wildlife along the way.

10. Maasai Market

No visit to Nairobi is complete without exploring this vibrant open-air market. Browse colorful Maasai beadwork, soapstone carvings, batik fabrics, and other authentic Kenyan crafts. Remember to bargain for the best prices!
